Dear Editor-in-Chief,

Please find enclosed our research manuscript titled \textbf{``Predictive Energy-Aware Contact Graph Routing for SmallSat Relay Networks,''} which we are submitting for consideration as an original research article for publication in \textit{Physical Communication}.

This study addresses a critical operational challenge in deep-space and planetary Delay-Tolerant Networks (DTN): the energy-blind routing decisions made by the standard Contact Graph Routing (CGR) protocol. While standard CGR relies on the deterministic predictability of orbital paths, it assumes that relay nodes have infinite energy reserves. This assumption is invalid for heterogeneous space relay networks containing energy-constrained SmallSats. Standard CGR overburdens small-scale satellites, causing rapid battery depletion to critical shutdown levels, which leads to catastrophic data loss and packet drops.

To solve this problem, we present two novel routing algorithms developed within a discrete-event Mars relay simulation network:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Energy-Aware CGR (ECGR):} A baseline algorithm that protects relay health by routing traffic dynamically based on the instantaneous state of charge (SoC) of relay batteries.
  \item \textbf{Predictive Energy and Capacity-Aware CGR (P-ECGR):} An advanced prediction-based extension that integrates analytical solar-charging predictions (based on future orbital eclipse intervals) and a reservation-based energy booking registry.
\end{enumerate}

By tracking committed bundle transmissions, P-ECGR prevents energy overbooking and effectively load-balances routing workloads across heterogeneous relay fleets. Extensive Monte Carlo simulations using SPICE ephemeris kernels show that both proposed algorithms sustain zero packet drops. P-ECGR elevates the bundle delivery ratio to $90.0 \pm 1.7\%$ (outperforming CGR's $83.7 \pm 8.9\%$), dramatically reduces delivery latency, and maintains battery health above critical thresholds (spending just $2.0 \pm 4.7\%$ of the time below $20\%$ SoC, compared to standard CGR's repeated deep discharges). Crucially, the routing computational complexity is minimal, executing in under $5\,$ms on flight-grade RAD750 space processors.

We believe that this research is highly relevant to the scope of \textit{Physical Communication}, particularly in the areas of satellite networks, space-based communications, and resource-constrained routing.
