## 📖 Abstract

As humanity expands its exploratory and commercial activities across cislunar, Martian, and deep space domains, the physical constraint of round-trip light-time latency renders terrestrial operational command loops functionally obsolete. To mitigate severe communication bottlenecks and dynamic data degradation, next-generation deep space missions will rely on edge-computing Artificial Intelligence (AI) to execute real-time telemetry prioritization, dynamic cognitive spectrum management, and autonomous edge data triage. However, delegating communication governance to edge-deployed autonomous systems introduces profound geopolitical, legal, and security vulnerabilities that existing international frameworks are fundamentally ill-equipped to govern.

This study conducts a comprehensive policy analysis of the regulatory and governance deficits induced by space-grade AI telecommunication systems. By modeling a mathematically simulated multinational Martian dust crisis, we demonstrate how standard utilitarian channel optimization algorithms systematically institutionalize bias, leading to techno-imperialist data triage and the marginalization of emerging space nations. We expand this analysis to map broader structural challenges: the threat of algorithmic neo-colonialism over closed-source neural weights, the accountability gap under Article VI of the Outer Space Treaty regarding non-deterministic "black-box" decision-making, and critical cybersecurity risks—including adversarial machine learning spectrum spoofing and ground-segment weight poisoning.

To secure the outer space commons and prevent escalatory spectral disputes, we propose a binding regulatory instrument: the **International Code of Practice for Autonomy in Deep Space Telecommunications**. Housed under the auspices of UN COPUOS and the ITU, this framework establishes mandates for Explainable AI (XAI) edge decision-logging, secure hardware-encrypted cryptographic data provenance, and a transition to cooperative, Rawlsian-guided dynamic spectrum allocation boundaries. Ultimately, we argue that the governance of deep space infrastructure must not be implicitly hardcoded by proprietary algorithms, but explicitly structured through international multilateral consensus.

## 🚀 Key Highlights & Research Pillars

### 1. The Dynamic Telemetry Triage Model (Section 4)
* **The Martian Dust Anomaly Scenario:** We model a multinational orbiter carrying a high-bandwidth Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR - Country A) and a low-bandwidth, transient climate spectrometer (Country B). During a global dust storm, available downlink bandwidth drops from **500 kbps to 50 kbps**.
* **Figure 1 (Decision Path):** A high-fidelity, customized TikZ diagram outlining the autonomous decision logic, showing how the AI Comm Manager acts as an edge triage filter.
* **Table 2 (Philosophical Comparison):** Compares Benthamite Utilitarianism, Rawlsian Maximin justice, and Sovereign Contractarian frameworks in algorithmic optimization.

### 2. Quantitative Performance & Policy Simulation (Section 4.3)
* **Figure 3 (Simulation Graph):** A vector-grade TikZ line plot depicting cumulative payload data survival rate (%) vs. available link bandwidth (kbps):
  * *Utilitarian Policy:* Aggressively deletes the spectrometer's data to maximize raw volume throughput from the radar, leading to **0% spectrometer data survival**.
  * *Rawlsian Maximin Policy:* Guarantees a baseline allocation to the spectrometer, ensuring **100% data survival** while dynamically compressing the radar telemetry down to 20% survival.

### 3. Broader Ethical Challenges (Section 5)
* **Algorithmic Neo-Colonialism:** The geopolitical monopoly of ground network infrastructure (DSN, Estrack, IDSN) and the strategic control of closed-source neural weights.
* **The OST Article VI Liability Gap:** Resolving explainability and legal accountability for non-deterministic AI decisions under international space law.
* **Securitization of Telemetry:** The dual-use nature of high-resolution space sensors and the strategic bias toward military intelligence over civilian environmental science during anomalous states.

### 4. Space Cybersecurity & Warfare Risks (Section 6)
* **Adversarial Machine Learning (Cognitive Jamming):** Spoofing techniques to trick dynamic reinforcement-learning-based radios into vacating strategic bands.
* **Supply-Chain Neural Weight Poisoning:** Injecting microscopic perturbations in terrestrial updates to trigger backdoors in edge spacecraft processors.
* **Spectral Crowding:** The risks of uncoordinated dynamic spectrum seizure by competing national space coalitions in lunar or Martian orbits.

### 5. Draft United Nations Resolution (Section 7)
* A complete, structured treaty draft of the proposed **International Code of Practice for Autonomy in Deep Space Telecommunications** under UN COPUOS, establishing edge auditability, cryptographic provenance, and ITU dynamic spectrum sharing registries.
